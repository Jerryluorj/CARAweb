Every Patient Deserves Treatment That's Right for Them

Modern clinical trials can now match each person with the treatment most likely to help them—based on their unique characteristics

The Traditional Approach: One Size Doesn't Fit All

In traditional clinical trials, patients are randomly assigned to treatments without considering their individual characteristics. This means some patients might receive treatments that aren't the best fit for them—even when doctors could have predicted a better match.

The result? Some patients don't get the help they could have received, and we miss opportunities to learn which treatments work best for which people.

The Solution: Personalized Clinical Trials (CARA Design)

CARA (Covariate-Adjusted Response-Adaptive) design is a smarter way to run clinical trials. It considers each patient's unique characteristics—like their biomarkers, medical history, or other health factors—and assigns them to the treatment most likely to benefit them.

The result? More patients receive treatments that work for them, while still gathering the scientific evidence needed to help future patients.

Real-World Example: Breast Cancer Treatment

HER2-Positive Breast Cancer Study

The Discovery: About 25-30% of breast cancer patients have overexpression of the HER2 gene, which makes tumors more aggressive.

The Treatment: Trastuzumab (Herceptin) works specifically for HER2-positive patients but not for HER2-negative patients.

With CARA Design:

  • HER2-positive patients are preferentially assigned to trastuzumab
  • HER2-negative patients receive alternative treatments
  • More patients get the right treatment from the start
  • We learn faster which combinations work best for each group

How a Patient Experiences CARA Design

Step 1: Enrollment & Testing

You join the trial. Doctors collect information about your biomarkers, medical history, and other health factors.

Step 2: Intelligent Assignment

The trial's algorithm considers your profile and what's been learned from previous patients. You're assigned to the treatment most likely to help someone like you.

Step 3: Treatment & Monitoring

You receive treatment and are closely monitored. Your response helps the trial learn and improve assignments for future patients.

Step 4: Continuous Learning

As the trial progresses, the system gets smarter, making even better matches for later patients based on accumulated knowledge.

Benefits for Everyone

For Patients

You have a better chance of receiving the treatment that will actually help you, rather than being randomly assigned to something that might not be the best fit for your specific situation.

For Future Patients

The trial learns which treatments work for which types of patients, creating a personalized medicine roadmap that will benefit countless people in the future.

For Medical Science

We discover not just whether a treatment works, but who it works for—leading to more targeted, effective treatments for different patient groups.

Ethical Care

More patients receive beneficial treatments during the trial itself, rather than being assigned to less effective options simply due to random chance.

Faster Results

By matching patients with treatments more effectively, trials can often reach conclusions more quickly with fewer participants.

Real Precision Medicine

This is precision medicine in action—using your individual characteristics to make better treatment decisions from day one.

This Is the Future of Clinical Trials

CARA design represents a fundamental shift in how we think about clinical trials. Instead of treating all patients the same, we recognize that everyone is unique. By considering your individual characteristics, we can increase your chances of receiving a treatment that will actually help you—while still conducting rigorous science.

Every patient in a clinical trial deserves the best possible care. That's what personalized trial design delivers.
